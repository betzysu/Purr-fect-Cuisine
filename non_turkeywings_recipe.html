Smothered Turkey Wings

Instead of the traditional roasted turkey for Thanksgiving, try smothered turkey wings! Serve over warm rice for a tasty dish!

prep time

20 mins

cook time

2 hrs 40 mins

serving

10 servings

instructions

step 1

Preheat oven to 350°F (180°C)

step 2

Rinse the wings and pat dry with paper towels. Mix all spices together in a small bowl. Sprinkle the spices over the turkey wings and toss the wings to coat evenly. Spray a large roasting pan with nonstick cooking spray.

step 3

Lay the wings in the pan. Add the onions, bell peppers, and butter into the pan. Cover the pan with aluminum foil and bake for 1 hour and 15 mins.

step 4

Remove pan from the oven and drain the pan drippings into a bowl or measuring cup. Place the wings back into the oven uncovered while you make the gravy.

step 5

For the Gravy: Use a fat separator cup or skim 1/2 cup of the fat off the top of the pan drippings. Reserve the rest of the drippings. Pour the fat into a pot and heat on medium high. Whisk in the flour for 2-3 minutes to cook out the flour taste. Pour in the chicken stock while whisking.

step 6

Then, pour in the remaining pan drippings. Add in the onion and garlic powders. Continue stirring until the gravy thickens; about 3 minutes. (Will thicken more in the oven). Remove from heat. Taste and adjust seasoning as desired. Pour gravy on top of the wings. Cover the pan with foil again. Bake for an additional 30-40 mins or until wings are tender and cooked completely.

ingredients

For the Turkey Wings

6-8 cut turkey wings

2 tablespoons poultry seasoning (Brand of your choice)

1-2 tablespoons Creole or Cajun seasoning (Brand of your choice)

1 tablespoon onion powder

1 tablespoon paprika

1 teaspoon garlic powder

½ teaspoon celery salt

1 tablespoon Accent seasoning (Optional)

1 onion sliced

1 bell pepper sliced

½ stick unsalted butter sliced thinly

For the Gravy

Pan drippings from wings

½ cup all purpose flour

3 cups chicken stock or broth

2 teaspoons onion powder

1 teaspoon garlic powder

Salt and pepper to taste

tools

Mixing Bowls

Whisk

Measuring Spoons

Measuring Cups
